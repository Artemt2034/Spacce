SPACE
relatively empty areas of the universe that lie outside the boundaries of the atmospheres of celestial bodies. Space is not completely empty space: it contains, albeit with a very low density, interstellar matter (mainly hydrogen molecules), oxygen in small quantities (remnant after the explosion of a star), cosmic rays and electromagnetic radiation, as well as hypothetical dark matter.
